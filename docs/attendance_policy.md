# Attendance Policy

## Minimum Attendance Threshold

Students must maintain a minimum attendance rate of **75%** to remain in good standing.
Students whose attendance falls below 75% are automatically flagged as **at-risk**.

## Attendance Status Definitions

- **Present** — Student attended the full session.
- **Absent** — Student did not attend with no prior notification.
- **Late** — Student arrived more than 10 minutes after the session start time.
- **Excused** — Absence approved in advance (medical certificate, family emergency, official school activity).

## Consequences of Low Attendance

| Attendance Rate | Status          | Action                                              |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| >= 90%          | Good Standing   | No action required                                  |
| 75% - 89%       | Acceptable      | Advisory notice to student                          |
| 60% - 74%       | At-Risk         | Written warning; mandatory counselling session      |
| < 60%           | Critical        | Parent/guardian notification; academic probation    |
| < 50%           | Failing         | Risk of grade retention; committee review required  |

## Excused Absence Procedures

1. Student or guardian must notify the school before or within 24 hours of the absence.
2. A medical certificate or official documentation must be submitted within 3 school days.
3. Excused absences do not count against the attendance rate but are tracked separately.
4. A maximum of 10 excused absences per semester is permitted without a committee review.

## Late Arrival Policy

- Arrivals within the first 10 minutes are recorded as Present.
- Arrivals between 10 and 30 minutes after start are recorded as Late.
- Three Late records in a single month are equivalent to one Absent for reporting purposes.
- Arrivals more than 30 minutes after start are recorded as Absent.

## Grading Impact

- Attendance is reviewed at the end of each grading period.
- Students below 75% attendance in a subject may be disqualified from sitting the final examination.
- Grade promotion requires a minimum overall attendance of 75% across all subjects.

## Reporting Periods

- Weekly reports cover Monday through Sunday.
- Monthly reports cover the calendar month.
- Semester reports are generated at the end of each academic semester.
- The at-risk threshold for all automated reports is 75% unless overridden by the AT_RISK_THRESHOLD system setting.
